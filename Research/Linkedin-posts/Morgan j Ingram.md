**Summary:**  
Morgan Ingram argues that many sales organizations are approaching AI adoption incorrectly by focusing solely on efficiency gains without defining how the saved time should be used. While he strongly supports AI and uses it extensively himself, he believes many teams are treating AI as a productivity solution rather than a capability-building tool.

He notes that as AI becomes standard across the industry, competitive advantage will no longer come from access to AI tools. Instead, differentiation will come from the quality of human interactions, business acumen, industry expertise, relationship building, and creative sales execution.

Morgan encourages sales leaders to intentionally invest the time AI creates into higher-value activities such as strategic account engagement, industry learning, community participation, networking, and in-person relationship building.

**Key Insight:**  
AI creates competitive advantage only when the time it saves is reinvested into developing uniquely human sales skills and deeper buyer relationships.
