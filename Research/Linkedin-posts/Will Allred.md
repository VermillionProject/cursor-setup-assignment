**Summary:**  
Will Allred explains that most sales outreach is designed for buyers who are already actively considering a solution, which limits overall opportunity creation. Using a buyer-awareness framework, he argues that sellers often focus too heavily on pitching products rather than understanding the prospect's current mindset.

He emphasizes that prospects are not primarily thinking about the seller or their product. Instead, effective prospecting starts by identifying and validating problems the buyer is experiencing. Rather than leading with product features or company information, salespeople should create demand by helping buyers recognize challenges they may not have fully considered.

Will suggests that demand creation messaging focused on pain points can significantly expand the number of opportunities available compared to simple product-centric outreach.

**Key Insight:**  
The most effective prospecting focuses on uncovering buyer pain and creating demand rather than immediately pitching products or services.
