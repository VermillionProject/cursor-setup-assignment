# Aaron Ross

## Post 1

**Date:** 1 Week Ago

**Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_i-built-the-outbound-machine-at-salesforce-activity-7468576311201026048-BWa4](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_i-built-the-outbound-machine-at-salesforce-activity-7468576311201026048-BWa4)

**Summary:**  
Aaron Ross reflects on his experience building Salesforce's outbound revenue engine and challenges the recurring claim that outbound sales is dead. He argues that when sales performance declines, teams often react by increasing volume, adding more outreach, more tools, more budget, and more pressure. While this occasionally works, it often accelerates existing problems.

He suggests that the solution is rarely more activity. Instead, successful teams focus on fewer, more intentional, and more human interactions. Aaron also warns that AI can amplify the temptation to scale ineffective processes rather than improving them.

**Key Insight:**  
When sales results decline, the answer is often not more activity but more deliberate, relationship-driven actions focused on quality over quantity.

## Post 2

**Date:** 2 Weeks Ago

**Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_the-deals-you-lose-arent-lost-to-your-competitor-activity-7466532426090086401-wco4](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_the-deals-you-lose-arent-lost-to-your-competitor-activity-7466532426090086401-wco4)

**Summary:**  
Aaron Ross argues that many deals are lost not because of competitors but because salespeople seek approval and avoid difficult conversations. Reps often hesitate to ask challenging qualification questions because they fear hearing "no" and losing potential opportunities. As a result, they keep weak deals alive, avoid direct conversations, and misinterpret buying signals.

According to Aaron, trust is built when salespeople are willing to risk rejection and tell prospects the truth, even if that truth may end the deal. The willingness to walk away from a poor fit often creates more credibility than trying to keep every opportunity alive.

**Key Insight:**  
The willingness to risk losing a deal is often what creates trust and reveals whether a genuine opportunity exists.

**Primary Topics:**

- Deal Qualification
- Sales Psychology
- Discovery
- Trust Building
- Prospecting
- Sales Mindset



## Post 3

**Date:** 2 Months Ago

**Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_ceocro-60-seconds-to-see-which-main-challenge-activity-7446580368461336576-9UgN](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aaronross_ceocro-60-seconds-to-see-which-main-challenge-activity-7446580368461336576-9UgN)

**Summary:**  
Aaron Ross shares a self-assessment tool designed for CEOs and CROs to identify their primary growth challenges. The framework categorizes common business obstacles into five areas: Strategy Fog, Pipeline Panic, Execution Friction, Phantom Revenue, and the AI Distraction Loop. The goal is to help revenue leaders diagnose underlying issues before pursuing solutions.

The post emphasizes that growth problems often stem from a small number of recurring patterns and that identifying the correct problem category is the first step toward improving revenue performance.

**Key Insight:**  
Revenue leaders often struggle because they misdiagnose the real problem. Effective growth starts with identifying the root challenge before applying solutions.

**Primary Topics:**

- Revenue Operations
- Pipeline Management
- Sales Leadership
- Growth Strategy
- AI in Business
- Revenue Diagnostics
