**Summary:**  
Chris Orlob shares a story from Chet Holmes, author of The Ultimate Sales Machine, about transforming a struggling advertising business through highly targeted account-based selling. Instead of pursuing thousands of potential customers, Holmes identified the small group of accounts responsible for the majority of industry revenue and focused exclusively on them.

His strategy relied on consistent, long-term outreach through multiple channels over several months, even when there was no immediate response. The persistence eventually led to major wins, including landing large enterprise accounts and dramatically increasing market share.

Chris uses this story to illustrate that winning high-value accounts often requires sustained, coordinated outreach rather than a handful of well-crafted emails. He argues that sellers should concentrate their efforts on a carefully selected group of strategic accounts and commit to a long-term engagement strategy.

**Key Insight:**  
High-value accounts are won through consistent, long-term account-based outreach rather than isolated sales activities or single cold email campaigns.
