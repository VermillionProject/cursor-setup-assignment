**Date:** 18/06/2026

**Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_im-doing-a-cold-call-workshop-for-brex-share-7473080582227480578-a5rh/](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_im-doing-a-cold-call-workshop-for-brex-share-7473080582227480578-a5rh/)

**Summary:**  
Josh Braun explains the "gumball machine" analogy for cold calling. He argues that a salesperson's job is not to chase positive outcomes such as meetings, but to consistently perform the activity itself. Rejections and unsuccessful calls are not evidence of poor performance. Instead, they are proof that the salesperson is putting in the required effort.

He also emphasizes separating outcomes from lessons. Rather than focusing on mistakes or failures, salespeople should identify what each interaction can teach them and use those insights to improve future performance.

**Key Insight:**  
Success in cold outreach comes from focusing on controllable actions rather than outcomes. Consistent effort and continuous learning matter more than individual results.

**Summary:**  
Josh Braun reflects on a lesson from yoga: two seemingly contradictory emotions or experiences can exist simultaneously. He explains that people can feel confident and uncertain, grateful and grieving, or afraid while still moving forward. The post challenges the idea that progress requires the absence of discomfort or pain.

He emphasizes that growth often involves holding opposing truths at the same time rather than trying to eliminate one of them. Progress comes from accepting tension instead of fighting it.

**Key Insight:**  
Personal and professional growth often requires accepting conflicting emotions rather than waiting for complete certainty or comfort.

## Post 3

**Date:** 17/06/2026

**Link:**  
[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_watch-two-reps-say-the-exact-same-thing-share-7472958659015278594-YIpN/](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_watch-two-reps-say-the-exact-same-thing-share-7472958659015278594-YIpN/)

**Summary:**  
Josh Braun compares two approaches to sales conversations: pitching versus questioning. Instead of presenting facts directly, he suggests framing observations as assumptions or questions that encourage prospects to respond. By making an intentionally optimistic assumption, prospects often correct the seller and reveal challenges in their own words.

The post highlights how effective sales conversations are driven by curiosity rather than persuasion. When prospects articulate problems themselves, they become more invested in addressing them.

**Key Insight:**  
Questions create engagement and self-discovery, while pitches often create resistance. The best sales conversations help buyers uncover their own problems.

**Primary Topics:**

- Cold Outreach
- Discovery Questions
- Sales Psychology
- Prospecting
- Consultative Selling
